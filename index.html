The gap & why now

The category generates complex biomarker data but lacks a governed way to interpret it in context — and the window is opening now.

The challenge is no longer producing numbers; it is interpreting them responsibly — across patient, clinic and measurement context, with explicit scientific authority and controlled communication. Five shifts are arriving at once — cheaper omics, growing clinics, tighter regulation, demand for defensible evidence and AI that cuts both ways — and none resolves without a physician-controlled interpretation layer.

Omics become affordable

Proteomics and multi-omics are falling in price and becoming clinically usable — data volume per patient explodes.

Longevity & healthspan clinics are growing

A new care category is emerging, with fragmented tools and no shared interpretation layer.

EU regulation

MDR/EHDS favor evidenced, accountable statements — and raise the barrier to entry for consumer players.

Demand for defensible interpretation

Clinics and patients want defensible interpretation, not hype-driven longevity. Trust becomes the differentiator.

AI raises capability and risk

AI increases both capability and risk — HADP constrains AI assistance through governed libraries, authority hierarchies and physician-controlled release gates.

Governed interpretive defensibility

Defensibility emerges from governed interpretation, structured architecture, physician-controlled workflows and accumulated knowledge.

A competitor can ship a feature in a sprint; it cannot retrofit how interpretation is produced, reviewed and released once that structure is the product. Features are copyable — the operating model is not.

Moat 01

Regulation by Design

Interpretation is produced inside the architecture — context, sources, authority hierarchies and release gates are structural, not features. Regulation-by-design becomes a moat rather than a burden.

Moat 02

The Interpretation Doctrine

The doctrine — Tri-State, CIS 0–5, Actionability A–E, Physician Gate, library-governed AI assistance, no unified score — is governance that can’t be retrofitted as a feature. It structures interpretation from the ground up.

Moat 03

Operational Clinical Integration

Once the methodology sits inside the physician review workflow, switching cost is high. White-label binds the clinic brand; the interpretation layer stays VitaBahn.

Moat 04

Contextual Interpretation Engine

Patient, clinic and measurement context are evaluated before interpretation — the layer competitors would have to rebuild wholesale, not bolt on.

Moat 05

Curated Knowledge Libraries

Curated, versioned scientific authorities and rules govern every interpretation — an accumulating, source-bound asset that compounds with use.

HADP Alpha

Governed interpretation in practice — already structured within HADP Alpha.

HADP Alpha combines biomarker values, longitudinal history, context and curated scientific authorities into structured interpretation — produced for physician review, lock and controlled release. A controlled demonstration record across six health domains, then one statement up close:

Patient Context

Age, sex, menopausal status, smoking, medication and accompanying conditions shape what a value means.

Clinic Context

Programme objectives and clinic setting frame how interpretation is scoped and communicated.

Measurement Context

Assay, laboratory, units, fasting and preparation are checked before any longitudinal comparison.

Scientific Authority Mapping

Every interpretation is bound to curated, versioned scientific authorities — not an open-ended model.
